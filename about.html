About Magnate Flora House

For over 25 years, we've been cultivating passion, knowledge, and the finest lavender varieties in the world.

Our Lavender Journey

Rooted in Passion, Growing with Purpose

Founded in 1995 by horticulturist Evelyn Magnate, Magnate Flora House began as a small family nursery specializing in Mediterranean herbs. Evelyn's particular fascination with lavender—its resilience, beauty, and myriad uses—soon transformed our focus entirely.

What started as a quarter-acre plot with just five lavender varieties has blossomed into a 50-acre lavender sanctuary and educational center. Today, we cultivate over 120 lavender varieties, conduct research on sustainable growing practices, and share our knowledge with gardeners worldwide.

Our mission has always been simple: to help people discover the joy of growing lavender and to promote its sustainable cultivation. We believe that lavender isn't just a plant—it's a way to connect with nature, reduce stress, and create beauty in our lives.

Our History

From humble beginnings to becoming a leading authority in lavender cultivation, our journey reflects our dedication to this remarkable plant.

1995

The Beginning

Evelyn Magnate establishes Magnate Flora House on a small family property, focusing initially on Mediterranean herbs before specializing in lavender.

2002

First Lavender Festival

We host our first annual Lavender Festival, attracting visitors from across the region and establishing our reputation as lavender specialists.

2008

Research Initiative

Launch of our lavender research program focusing on drought-resistant varieties and organic cultivation methods.

2015

Expansion and Education

Opening of our Visitor Center and Educational Gardens, offering workshops, tours, and certification programs in lavender cultivation.

2020

Sustainable Practices

Implementation of comprehensive sustainable farming practices, achieving organic certification for all our lavender fields.

2023

Global Recognition

Recipient of the International Horticultural Society's "Excellence in Specialty Cultivation" award for our work with rare lavender varieties.

Our Guiding Principles

Sustainable Cultivation

We practice and promote organic, regenerative farming methods that nurture soil health and protect local ecosystems.

Education & Sharing

We believe knowledge should be shared. Through workshops, online resources, and one-on-one consultations, we empower gardeners at all levels.

Biodiversity Preservation

We maintain a living collection of rare and heirloom lavender varieties, ensuring their preservation for future generations.

Community Connection

We actively collaborate with local communities, schools, and organizations to promote gardening as a source of wellbeing.
