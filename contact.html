Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best time to plant lavender?

The ideal time to plant lavender is in the spring after the last frost, or in early fall in warmer climates. This gives the plants time to establish roots before extreme temperatures. In colder regions, spring planting is recommended to allow plants to establish before winter.

Do you offer lavender gardening workshops?

Yes! We offer monthly workshops on various lavender gardening topics, including propagation, pruning, harvesting, and using lavender in crafts and cooking. Workshops are held on the second Saturday of each month at our nursery. Check our website for upcoming topics and registration information.

Can I visit your display gardens?

Absolutely! Our display gardens are open to the public during business hours. We have over 50 varieties of lavender arranged in thematic gardens, including a fragrance garden, culinary lavender garden, and drought-tolerant landscape display. Admission is free, and our staff is available to answer questions.

Do you ship plants outside of your local area?

Yes, we ship lavender plants nationwide during appropriate planting seasons. We carefully package plants to ensure they arrive in healthy condition. Shipping is available from March through June and September through November. We recommend checking our website for specific shipping dates and zones.

What lavender varieties do you recommend for beginners?

For beginners, we recommend English lavender varieties like 'Munstead' or 'Hidcote' as they are hardy, adaptable, and have a lovely fragrance. For warmer climates, Spanish lavender or French lavender varieties may be more suitable. Our gardening hotline can provide personalized recommendations based on your specific climate and soil conditions.
